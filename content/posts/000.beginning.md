This is the 4th day I've started animating. I want to achieve some confidence in this craft in 12 weeks. At the moment it is kind of the 10th time I'm "starting". Previous times I couldn't discipline myself to keep going. I hope this time I will do it. I developed some discipline before, so now it should be easier.

First day I watched a couple of tutorials, and downloaded H22. Initially I wanted to animate in grease pencil, but [I really suck at drawing](https://www.instagram.com/kono_kimochi/). So I decided to switch to 3d animation and maybe do some drawing animation later. Kind of increase difficulty.

Second day I animated 2 versions of a bouncing ball. It was really hard and boring. Didn't keep up with curves and layers. Reported a couple of bugs. Got a ball to record some references.

Third day I recorded several videos of a bouncing ball and vibe coded [a tool](https://github.com/mishazawa/vtrail) which draws the trail of a moving object on video. [It is my usual way of procrastinating.](https://github.com/mishazawa/my_slap_comp/commits/main/) Surprisingly, on the 3rd day it was very easy and intuitive to animate using curves. I made one animation of a bouncing ball from my reference and made a good-looking render. Tried to deploy the blog to neocities, but it doesn't allow hosting mp4 and webm.

Fourth day I took another reference and animated a bouncing ball with squash and stretch and rotation. Found a fun formula to make believable rotation:

```
(distance/radius) x (180/pi)
```

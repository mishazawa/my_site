Fifth day I spent a lot of time on an old project and watching tutorials on the `overlapping action` topic. In the evening I just made an ∞ shape path. Btw, it was harder than I thought. Even just to visualize the trajectory in 2 dimensions. Also I tried to figure out how to make a seamless loop when animating.

Sixth day I continued to work on overlapping action. This time I planned to make several exercises:

- block w/ pendulum
- block w/ pendulum with joints
- ball w/ tail (squirrel)

{{ video(path="001.webm", fallback="001.mp4") }}
{{ video(path="002.webm", fallback="002.mp4") }}
{{ video(path="003.webm", fallback="003.mp4") }}

This was hard as fuck. I constantly got stuck on the loop when trying to make the movement physically deterministic and correct, while forgetting that animation is an artistic activity and the thing should look believable, not correctly simulated.

Figured out a couple of things:

1. Offset timing/frames (stiffness) - how fast motion propagates; how stiff the material is for initial speed up and inertia.
2. Offset amplitude (damping) - how fast energy dissipates.

Pure physics, but you really need to see the motion, not just know the formula.
